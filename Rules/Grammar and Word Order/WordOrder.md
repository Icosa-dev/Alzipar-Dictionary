The basic sentence structure of the formal literary language is that of VSO ("Ate sam apples"). However informal and common speech may often take the form of SVO ("Sam ate apples"). 

The internal order of descriptors is as follows:
1. Quantity
2. Head Noun
3. Opinion or Evaluation
4. Size
5. Age
6. Form or Shape
7. Color
8. Origin, Material, or Purpose

For example to describe "Three tasty large, fresh, black coffees" in the language would be *Tirah Kopiun Lekin Azimin Frisk Iswed* which translates literally to "Three coffee tasty big fresh black".
