# Alzapar Morphology: Verb Forms

Verbs formed from a triconsonantal root (1-2-3) which general meaning (X) may take the following forms:

|   Form   |   Past Tense (Default)     |   Present Tense   |   Meaning                       |
|----------|----------------------------|-------------------|---------------------------------|
|   (V0)   |           N/A              |        N/A        |   Irregular non-rooted verb     |
|   (V1)   |          1a2a3a            |      Ya12u3u      |   To do X                       |
|   (V2)   |          1ā2a3a            |      Yu1ā2i3u     |   To do X unto something        |
|   (V3)   |          'A12a3a           |      Yu12i3u      |   To make someone do X          |
|   (V4)   |          Ta1a2a3a          |      Yata1a2a3u   |   To do X unto oneself          |
|   (V5)   |          Ta1ā2a3u          |      Yata1ā2a3u   |   To do X unto one another      |
|   (V6)   |          In1a2a3a          |      Yan1a2i3u    |   To be X-ed                    |
|   (V6)   |          I12a3a            |      Ya12ā3a      |   To become X                   |
|   (V7)   |          Ista12a3a         |      Yasta12i3u   |   To ask (someone) to do X      |

The first form (V1) can also act as an infinitive form or verbal noun form. Adverbs derived from the root take the form "1a23ī".

Adding the "sā" or "mā" particle before a past tense verb puts it in the future or perfect tense respectivly.
